America


American mythology is not as grand and majestic as the well-known classics such as Greek mythology. However, every mythology derived from collections of folklore stories, and American mythology is no exception. Each region and/or state of the United States has their own little collection of folklores and legends, some deriving as early as the 1700s colonists settled when the ago but plenty of them are from more recent times. To note, while some are influenced by Native American tradition, these folklores are not exactly from the actual tribal reliefs of the community native people. Rather, American folklore consists of tall tales, popular beliefs, local legends and myths, oral-told stories, etc.

The Philippines


I personally chose this country's mythology to research because I am fond of my filipino heritage. In addition, I am intrigued at some of the Philippines' mythical creatures. Many stories and legends that they come from are from the indigenous Filipino folk religions, many of its ethical faiths influencing one another. Many of its folklore is also influenced by Indonesian and Malay myths as well as Muslim, Hindu, Shinto, Bhuddist, and Christian faiths. Like Greek mythology, these stories are meant to explan the natural world. Such stories are passed down through oral tradition and preserved through community shamans, community elders, and other spiritual leaders. Historically, the term for mythologies and indigenous religions have been referred to as Anitism, meaning "ancestral religion".
